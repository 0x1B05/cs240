\problem{}
You are given an $n \times n$ grid, and a procedure $V(i,j)$ that assigns an integer value to each position $(i,j)$ in the grid, where $i$ and $j$ are integers such that $1 \le i,j \le n$. Your goal is to find a local minimum (or sink) in the grid (i.e., integers $i^{\star}, j^{\star}$ with $1 \le i^{\star}, j^{\star} \le n$ such that for all neighbors $(i,j)$ of $(i^{\star},j^{\star})$ in the grid, $V(i^{\star},j^{\star}) \le V(i,j)$). The neighbors of $(i,j)$ are $(i-1,j)$, $(i+1,j)$, $(i,j+1)$, $(i,j-1)$; the elements along the diagonals do not count
as neighbors.

Design an algorithm that makes $O(n)$ calls to $V$. Note that the grid has $n^{2}$ nodes.
